The Critical Edition of the Mahabharata is not a single storytelling voice. It is four nested frames of transmission — each narrator recounting what a previous narrator said — layered across generations of oral tradition.

The Four Boundaries

The Critical Edition spans two works — the Mahābhārata and the Harivamsha — defined by four textual boundaries. B2 and B3 mark the seam between the two.

Mahābhārata CE · 18 books, 95 sections Harivamsha CE · 1 book, 3 sections

The Harivamsha is the khilā (appendix) of the Mahabharata in the Critical Edition — not classified as a separate 19th book, but listed within the same table of contents. The relationship between B2 and B3 — an ending and a beginning — is explored in the essay "4 Boundaries of the Mahābhārat".
